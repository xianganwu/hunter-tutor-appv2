## PART 1 — VERBAL REASONING

> *The Verbal Reasoning section has two question types: Synonyms (pick the word closest in meaning) and Sentence Completions (pick the word that best fills the blank). On the ISEE there are 40 questions in 20 minutes.*

### About Synonym Questions
Each question asks for the word **most nearly the same** in meaning. Focus on the root meaning, not the context of use.

### About Sentence Completion Questions
Each question has one blank. Choose the word that best fits the meaning and tone of the sentence.

## PART 2 — QUANTITATIVE REASONING

> *Quantitative Reasoning tests mathematical thinking, not calculation. Section has two types: Word Problems (Q1–20) and Quantitative Comparisons (Q21–37). In QC questions, you compare Column A to Column B.*
>
> **QC Answer Choices always:**
> - (A) Column A is greater
> - (B) Column B is greater
> - (C) The two quantities are equal
> - (D) The relationship cannot be determined from the information given

---

### Official ERB Sample Questions — Quantitative Reasoning

#### Numbers and Operations

**Sample 1:** Chris has a basket that contains only red and green apples. 40% of the apples in the basket are red. If there are 6 red apples, how many green apples are in the basket?

- (A) 4
- (B) 6
- (C) 8
- **(D) 9** ✓

*Explanation: 40% = 6 red → total = 6 ÷ 0.40 = 15. Green = 15 − 6 = 9.*

---

#### Quantitative Comparison — Numbers and Operations

**Sample 2:**

| Column A | Column B |
|----------|----------|
| √0.81 | √8.1 |

- (A) Column A is greater
- **(B) Column B is greater** ✓
- (C) The two quantities are equal
- (D) Cannot be determined

*Explanation: √0.81 = 0.9; √8.1 ≈ 2.85. Column B is greater.*

---

#### Algebraic Concepts

**Sample 3:** Jack and Michael both ride bikes along the same path. Jack starts at one end and bikes toward Michael at a speed of 30 miles per hour. Michael starts at the other end, 90 miles away, and bikes toward Jack at a speed of 60 miles per hour. Which equation represents how long in minutes, M, it will take them to meet?

- (A) 30M + 60M = 90
- **(B) 90M − 1,000 = 30M** ✓
- (C) 30M = 60M − 90
- (D) 30 + 60 = 90M

*Note: Answer is B — uses minutes as the variable in the rate equation.*

---

#### Quantitative Comparison — Algebra

**Sample 4:**

| Column A | Column B |
|----------|----------|
| x, where x/4 + 5 = 25 | y, where 6y + 11 = 77 |

- **(A) Column A is greater** ✓
- (B) Column B is greater
- (C) The two quantities are equal
- (D) Cannot be determined

*Explanation: x/4 = 20, x = 80. 6y = 66, y = 11. 80 > 11.*

---

#### Quantitative Comparison — Geometry

**Sample 5:** A solid figure has the following net (unfolded shape — a cross shape made of 6 squares of equal size, where the center column has 4 squares and the middle row has 3 squares forming a cross).

| Column A | Column B |
|----------|----------|
| Volume of the solid figure (in cm³) | 48 cm³ |

- (A) Column A is greater
- **(B) Column B is greater** ✓
- (C) The two quantities are equal
- (D) Cannot be determined

*Explanation: The net forms a rectangular prism. The volume is less than 48 cm³.*

---

#### Quantitative Comparison — Measurement

**Sample 6:** Triangle PQR has sides of length 3, 4, and 5. Triangle STU is similar to triangle PQR, with the shortest side of STU equal to 9.

| Column A | Column B |
|----------|----------|
| Perimeter of Triangle STU | 50 |

- (A) Column A is greater
- **(B) Column B is greater** ✓
- (C) The two quantities are equal
- (D) Cannot be determined

*Explanation: Scale factor = 9/3 = 3. STU sides = 9, 12, 15. Perimeter = 36. 36 < 50.*

---

#### Quantitative Comparison — Data Analysis/Probability

**Sample 7:** A basketball team played 16 games this season. The histogram shows the number of points scored in each game, with bars for ranges 41–50, 51–60, 61–70, 71–80.

| Column A | Column B |
|----------|----------|
| The measure of central tendency of the points scored that could NEVER equal any individual score | Mean |

- **(A) Column A is greater** ✓ *(The median could NEVER equal any individual score)*
- (B) Column B is greater
- (C) The two quantities are equal
- (D) Cannot be determined

*Explanation: With 16 data points (even number), the median is the average of the 8th and 9th scores, which fall in different ranges — so the median cannot equal any actual score in the data set. Column A = Median, which is the measure that could never equal an individual score.*

---

#### Data Analysis/Probability — Spinner Question

**Sample 8:** Spinner 1 has 4 equal sections (1, 2, 3, 4). Spinner 2 has 3 equal sections (1, 2, 3). Both are spun and their results are added.

| Column A | Column B |
|----------|----------|
| Probability the sum = 3 | Probability the sum = 6 |

- (A) Column A is greater
- (B) Column B is greater
- **(C) The two quantities are equal** ✓
- (D) Cannot be determined

*Explanation: There are 4 × 3 = 12 possible outcomes. Sum = 3: (1+2, 2+1) = 2 ways → 2/12 = 1/6. Sum = 6: (3+3, 4+2) = 2 ways → 2/12 = 1/6. Equal.*

### Essay Tips for Hunter HCHS

The Hunter 2026 Writing Assignment differs slightly from the ISEE essay — it is longer and evaluated more rigorously by Hunter faculty. Key strategies:

4. **Show your thinking.** Hunter values intellectual curiosity. Ask yourself "why does this matter?" and answer it.
5. **Write legibly.** Your essay is handwritten and must be readable.
6. **Vocabulary matters.** Use precise words — but only words you know well.
